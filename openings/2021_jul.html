Open PhD Position

UC3Mâ€™s Computer Security (COSEC) group invites applications from highly motivated candidates for a fully funded Ph.D. position in the area of static code analysis tools for security and privacy. The position is funded by the H2020 project TRUST aWARE. The project's mission is to provide an effective security and privacy framework integrating tools and services adapted to the needs of citizens and stakeholders (CERTs, DPAs, organizations and developers) to identify, audit, analyze, prevent, and mitigate the impact of the security and privacy threats associated with citizen's digital activities in a timely manner, while enhancing software trust and regulatory compliance. The tools will be designed and developed collaboratively by integrating the requirements and operational challenges of each stakeholder.

The successful candidate will work under the supervision of Professor Juan Tapiador and is expected to:

  • design and implement static code analysis tools and APIs,
  • perform measurements and large-scale studies on the security and privacy of software for different platforms,
  • integrate existing software analysis tools into a comprehensive framework,
  • write up documentation and other project deliverables,
  • prepare demonstrators,
  • attend project meetings, and
  • deliver technical presentations.

What is funded

The Ph.D. position at UC3M is fully covered (€1,975 gross per month). This salary includes social and health benefits. The group also provides funding for registration and travel expenses to attend industrial and academic conferences in the area. At least one fully-funded internship abroad is expected during the Ph.D. The payment is made on a monthly basis.

Duration

The contract will be for one year, renewable for up to two additional years based on performance.

Eligibility

Candidates must hold a B.Sc. in areas related to computer science or telecommunications engineering. Postgraduate studies (holding a M.Sc. or being currently enrolled in one) will be a plus. The candidate must have a high command of English, both verbal and written, and will work in a highly international environment. The candidate must be ready to join UC3M as soon as possible, and no later than September, 2021.

Software analysis or data analysis skills are recommended, especially in the area of static and dynamic analysis of software, binary analysis and reverse engineering. Good coding skills (C/C++, Java, and Python) and experience in (or an interest for working in) the area of cybersecurity while conducting practical research are also desired. Good presentation skills will be a plus.

How to apply

Applicants interested in the position should submit their application here (call code 2021/170). The deadline for applications is July 22, 2021. Review of applications will begin immediately.

For enquiries about the position, please contact by email Juan Tapiador.
